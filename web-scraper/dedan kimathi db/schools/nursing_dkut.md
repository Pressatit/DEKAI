
# School of nursing

The Bachelor of Science Nursing programme is designed to empower students with the knowledge, skills and attitudes necessary to provide leadership in professional nursing practice, education, health care administration and participation in research. The graduates will be enabled to develop and integrate critical thinking and problem solving skills into the practice of nursing.
The program has had continued growth in student enrollment and staffing as well as classroom, offices, laboratory and other infrastructural expansion. Several cohorts have now graduated and are out in the market, both locally and internationally. To date(June 2023) three hundred and thirty-seven (337) students have graduated with Bachelor of Science in Nursing Degree.
Currently there are four cohorts of BScN-Upgrading and five cohorts of direct entry students, with a combined population of four hundred and nine (409) students. DeKUT, School of Nursing produces all-round, highly competent graduates ready to excel in the diverse working environments.

The dean of School of nursing is Dr. Florence Mbuthia

**School of nursing Vision**
“To propel healthcare to utmost height through excellence in nursing education, research, consultancy and community service”.
**School of nursing Mission**
To prepare highly skilled nursing professionals capable of providing health care services in diverse settings in the country and beyond; carry out and participate in innovative research; provide consultancy and undertake community service through:
  1. Building partnerships with health care institutions to improve quality of nurse training
  2. Promotion of a conducive environment for the training of professional nurses
  3. Enhancement of performance of practicing nurses by offering opportunity for professional development through continuing education
  4. Provision of academic leadership in research to generate evidence for cost effective health care interventions


**School of nursing Objectives**
  1. Provide quality nursing education through research-oriented practice and public service
  2. Ensure staff competence through advancement in knowledge and skills
  3. Establish partnerships with other health care institutions (both within the country and internationally) for knowledge sharing and skills transfer


**About School of Nursing**
The School of Nursing is one of the Schools in Dedan Kimathi University of Technology committed to training nursing students by equipping them with the knowledge, skills and attitudes necessary to provide leadership in professional nursing practice, education, health care administration and participation in research.
The school is committed to achieving its vision, “To propel healthcare to utmost height through excellence in nursing education, research, consultancy and community service”.
The school’s mission is “To prepare highly skilled nursing professionals capable of providing health care services in diverse settings in the country and beyond; carry out and participate in innovative research; provide consultancy and undertake community service”.
The school comprises of four departments: Medical-Surgical Nursing, Midwifery/Reproductive Health Nursing, Community Health Nursing and Nursing Education, Leadership and Management and Research. The departments are comprised of staff with specific relevant and varied specialties required for training competent nurses who are much needed to offer services in the region and beyond.

**History of DeKUT Nursing Program**
The Bachelor of Science in nursing course at Dedan Kimathi University of Technology had its initial batch of students in June 2011. Kimathi University College of Technology(KUCT), then a constituent college of JKUAT, started with the Bachelor of Science in Nursing (Upgrading) for in-service Diploma in Nursing (KRCHN) holders. The pioneer class had ten (10) students. Later, the following year in September 2012, the first class of direct entry students was admitted. The pioneer group of direct entry students comprised thirty-one (31) students. The program has had continued growth in student enrollment and staffing as well as classroom, offices, laboratory and other infrastructural expansion. Several cohorts have now graduated and are out in the market, both locally and internationally. To date (March 2025) four hundred and sixty-eight (468) students have graduated with Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BscN) Degree. Currently there are three cohorts of BscN-Upgrading and five cohorts of direct entry students, with a combined population of four hundred and ninety-six (496) students. The Bachelor of Science in Nursing course at Dedan Kimathi University of Technology, with its thorough theoretical, laboratory and clinical skills and through partnership with the Nyeri, Laikipia, and Kirinyaga together with the Ministry of Health, the Nursing council of Kenya (NCK), and other partners, produces all-round, highly competent graduates ready to excel in the diverse working environments.

# School of Nursing programmes
Dedan Kimathi University of Technology (DeKUT), School of Nursing, offers a Bachelor of Science Nursing program that aims to equip students with the necessary knowledge, skills, and attitudes for leadership roles in nursing practice, education, healthcare administration, and research. Graduates of the program are prepared to demonstrate critical thinking, problem-solving abilities, and integration of these skills into nursing practice. The program has experienced growth in both student enrollment and staffing, leading to infrastructural expansion in terms of classrooms, offices, and laboratories. Graduates of the School of Nursing have been successful in the job market, both locally and internationally, showcasing their competence and versatility. The School is also in the process of developing a Master of Science in Nursing program with various specialties, such as critical care, renal, medical-surgical nursing, mental health and psychiatric nursing, and reproductive health nursing. This initiative demonstrates DeKUT’s commitment to advancing nursing education and producing highly skilled professionals who can excel in various healthcare settings.
**Current programmes** 
The School of Nursing offers two programmes
  1. Bachelor of Science in Nursing (Upgrading)
  2. Bachelor of Science in Nursing (Direct)


**Bachelor of Science in Nursing (UPGRADING)**
**Minimum Entry Requirements of Bachelor of Science in Nursing (UPGRADING)**
The applicant must be a Kenya Registered Community Health Nurse (KRCHN) OR Kenya Registered Nurse/ Midwife (KRN/KRM/PH). Must be currently registered with the Nursing Council of Kenya and posses a valid Nursing Council of Kenya practice license with a minimum of 2 years nursing experience. In addition the candidate must possess Either :
i. A Minimum entry of C ( plain) in KCSE ii. Division II in KCE
**Mode of Study**
Part Time
**Duration**
2½ (Two and half ) Years

**Bachelor of Science in Nursing (DIRECT ENTRY)**
**Minimum Entry Requirements of Bachelor of Science in Nursing(DIRECT ENTRY)**
A minimum grade of C+ in KCSE with a C+ in Biology/Biological Sciences, English/Kiswahili, Mathematics/ physics and Chemistry

Equivalent qualifications as recognized by the University Senate.
**Mode of Study**
Full Time
**Duration**
4 years
