

# DeKUT Library
Welcome to Dedan Kimathi University of Technology University Library. Dedan Kimathi University of Technology University Library is one of the few Universities in the country that is privileged to have adopted and enjoying a wide range of innovative services and systems that are used to necessitate easy access and maximum utilization of library information resources.  The library’s primary responsibility is to assist students and academic staff in the process of transforming information into knowledge; it does these by providing information that is essential for the purpose of teaching, learning, innovation and research. The Library has wide access to information materials that are geared to support teaching, learning, research, and innovation activities for students, academic staff, researchers and the community at large. Currently, the library provides access to approximately 60,000 volumes of print resources, 20,000 databases of electronic journals and over 500,000 electronic books.

Dedan Kimathi University library has two libraries, the Main Campus Library (Nyeri) and the Nairobi CBD-Pension Towers 9th Floor. The Libraries are open for all DeKUT bonafide students, staff, and researchers.  As an information center, we are pleased to serve you and assist you in all your academic endeavors.

The University's Librarian is Dr. Fredrick Otike

# Dedan Kimathi University Library rules and regulations
Rules And Regulations
All library users must produce their identification as required during the service period.
Order and silence must be maintained at all times in the library. Smoking, eating, drinking and sleeping in the library is prohibited.
Briefcases, bags, overcoats, hats, umbrellas are not allowed in the library and should be deposited at the luggage area at the user’s own risk.
Loan procedures must be adhered to before any library and information materials are taken out of the library. An attempt to take improperly loaned materials will be treated as theft and appropriate disciplinary measures will be taken against the offenders.
All persons leaving the library must show all their documents to the security staff at the exit.
Use of mobile phones or any devices likely to cause disturbance in the library is prohibited. Ink bottles, paint and other electronic gadgets which may accidentally damage library material and other facilities are not allowed in the library. 
Any lost or mutilated information materials on loan will be replaced at the full cost of the material (current cost) plus 20 per cent administrative charges.
Fine of ksh. 10 per day per item is charged for all overdue of short-loan materials and Long-Loan Materials.
The right to borrow may be withdrawn until all books have been returned and any outstanding fines have been paid.
All registered members of the library must clear with the university library at the expiry of their membership.
There is a copyright protection law on most of the library materials. Deliberate infringement on this law e.g. unauthorized copying, is a criminal offence.
However, permissible limits of not more than a quarter of a book may be made for academic purposes. All library users must observe these limits in the library premise or elsewhere.
NB: The library reserves the right to withdraw library use rights on the basis of violation of set guidelines
